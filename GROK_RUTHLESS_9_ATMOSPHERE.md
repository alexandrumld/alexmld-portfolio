## DEEP-THINK

1. Does the dark feel like a SPACE or just a COLOR? Awwwards dark sites have depth — you feel like you're IN a space, not looking at a flat black rectangle. Does this site achieve that?
2. Is the grain RIGHT? .055 opacity, 160px tile, overlay blend. Is this the right amount? Too much (noisy)? Too little (invisible)? Should it animate (scroll-driven)?
3. Is the vignette TOO MUCH or JUST RIGHT? 4 radial gradients. Does it create depth or does it feel heavy?
4. Are the section backgrounds DISTINCT ENOUGH? bg:#070707 vs bg-alt:#0b0b0b — is this enough variation? Should the sections feel more different from each other?
5. Is the silver accent WORKING? #b8b8b8 is colder/more technical than gold. Is it doing its job? Should it be slightly warmer? Slightly cooler? Is it used consistently?
6. Is there enough LAYERING? Awwwards sites layer: background → gradient → content → overlay. Does this site have enough layers?
7. Does the atmosphere CHANGE as you scroll? Or is it static? Should the grain/vignette shift? Should there be per-section atmosphere?
8. Is the overall MOOD RIGHT? The site should feel: cold, technical, premium, cinematic, personal. Does it? Or does it feel generic dark?
